Summary

Complex prescription apps are contributing to epidemic levels of non-adherence. Sphere was designed with a goal of seeing a measurable increase in adherence and improvement of patient health.

Over the years I’ve had my share of poor experiences that resulted in missing medication. Misses which impacted tests months down the road. In early 2018 I took this on as a personal project to explore solutions that could repair the broken pharmacy experience.

Problem

Over 50% of patients do not take their prescriptions, and 30% fail to fill them at all. Resulting in 30%-50% of treatment failures, hospitalization, and 125,000 deaths per year.

Solution

Use of mobile pill reminders improves adherence by a minimum of 12.3%. An efficient and transparent pharmacy creates an additional increase in adherence.

User Survey

Competitive Analysis & Target Market

CVS Pharmacy, Walgreens, and Kaiser Permanente market their apps to the growing senior population in the United States. However, recent findings suggest present-day solutions to mobile digital health does not improve the health of seniors. According to research a younger demographic is more likely to adopt health apps. Reducing Spheres target demographic to 25–45 allows for the adoption of the app in the short term while still positioning Sphere towards the growing senior population long term.

User stories and flows

To properly simplify processes for patients I asked myself two important questions:

  • What was keeping people from using health products?
  • For people who once used them, why did they stop?

A study conducted by the NCBI discovered time to manually enter data, hidden costs, and apps being confusing to use as the top reasons for non-use or discontinuing use. Therefore, I created stories and flows with a goal of reducing processes down to the bare minimum. Adding on carefully and only when necessary.

Sketching and low-fidelity

Giving users the ability to experience pill reminders without an account would help build trust. However, if one of the simplest features didn’t work properly there would be no reason to onboard and use Sphere as their pharmacy.

I gathered feedback from potential users on the lofi pill reminders, onboarding, and main navigation screens. Finding a need for:

  • Foundation - app relies on trust, timing, and managing expectations.
  • Reminder details - frequency (daily/weekly), times per day, units (e.g.tablets capsules), and ability to mark as taken from their lock screen.
  • Security - how their personal and medical information is being protected. Compliant with HIPPA
  • Clear communication - when pharmacists are available for live chat and what their camera is being used for.

A/B Testing

I ran an A/B test on the Today and Prescriptions screens to assist in guiding the design of other aspects of the app. Option B was chosen 80% of the time, however, I took note of strong aspects of the white design along with issues I spotted to create a stronger more cohesive design. Some of the highlights are below:

1.

Cards - Users preferred the more rounded cards for feeling more friendly and trustworthy.

2.

Blue header - Users said the blue header kept the page grounded with stronger hierarchy. Allowing users to keep attention on the dashboard.

3.

Button layout - Placement of buttons were more reachable touch targets, but blocked additional prescriptions from being displayed.

Patient and doctor names - Issue with perfect data. Half the content below the prescription title would break the design. In addition, there might be an opportunity to group patients.

3.

Prescription names - Longer medication names will run into button. Will it truncate or drop to two lines?

Usability testing & iterations

I tested in person with potential patients in tandem with design critiques. I found a need for the following:

  • New medication - Screen for infomation when adding new medications
  • Consistent pill reminder - Less jarring pill reminder screen integrated better with the rest of the application.
  • Errors - A way to resolve errors when scanning prescriptions
  • Family - Users are confused about family tab. Need clearer flow for users to add and manage family rembers.

Conclusion

Working on Sphere Pharmacy forced me to think about the impact of my design decisions. One of the most valuable insights I had was to be ok with not getting it right the first time. By failing earlier you can reduce how you impact users. Being willing to continously cycle back through with users and stay truly interested in how your design breaks is crucial. They’re not only helping you become a better more effective designer, but showing you what they need to lead healthier and happier lives.

I also learned a lot about maintaining consistency and communication throughout a product. Not only does consistency ease user expectations through flows it builds trust and makes an app worth using.

Lastly, although this was a personal project I learned a lot about not working in a silo. I made it point to reach out to my community of designers for critique and communicating with users in order to drive the product. The team focused mindset is really what guiding me to stronger solutions.
