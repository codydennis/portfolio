Summary

Improving patient adhearence and health

Complex prescription apps are contributing to the skyrocketing rate of patient non-adhearence. From unclear feedback to late deliveries, poor design in digital pharmacies has life threatening consequences.

Over the years I’ve had my share of poor experiences that resulted in missing medication. Misses which impacted tests months down the road. In early 2018 I took this on as a personal project to explore solutions that could repair the broken pharmacy experience.

Problem

Patient non-adherence

Over 50% of patients do not take their prescriptions and 30% fail to fill them at all. Resulting in 30%-50% of treatment failures, hospitalization, and 125,000 deaths per year.

Solution

Pill reminders & simple pharmacy

Use of mobile pill reminders improves adherence by a minimum of 12.3%. Integration of an efficient and transparent pharmacy results in an additional increase in adherence.

User Survey

7/10 Americans take prescriptions

Research gathered from my survey of 55 participants and supplemental studies show the average American takes at least one prescription, is non-adherent 1–4 times a month, prefers non-physical contact with pharmacists, and wants clear communication during the order process.
